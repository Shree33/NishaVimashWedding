Product Management Intern - Innovations

LogMeIn Innovations is a special team focused on finding the next moon shot product for LogMeIn. Over the course of three months, I worked with a small team of product managers, UX designers, software engineers, and data scientists to validate user problems and design product solutions using agile methodology and lean startup thinking. One of these product that we developed and tested over the summer is Scopist.io. Scopist is a new AI bot which will record your meetings and capture the key decisions, facts, and action items so that you can focus on collaboration during the meeting. During my time, I analyzed user research to create journey maps and personas to understand user habits and hone in on the key pain points that our new product would solve. I also conducted market research to understand the product's position in the field , estimate pricing, and identify opportunity areas for growth. Finally, I wrote new user stories and led grooming sessions to focus development on high priority targets with the help of the product manager and the engineering team.

  • Company: LogMeIn
  • Date: June 2017

Co-Founder

In the Fall of 2015, My friend, Finn, and I recognized an opportunity in the Tufts late night dining scene. The school sponsored late night option, "The Commons", was closed for the semester for improvements. We realized that students wanted a place to fill up on carbs and socialize with friends after going out for the night so we devised a rudimentary business plan, bought a fryer and ingredients, and opened our doors to the population. It was our first foray and we slowly learned how to entice customers and spread the word, how to set prices and specials to increase traffic, and how to get feedback from customers and turn them into return customers. Overall, we made a sizeable profit of our initial investment but we decided it was not worth competing against the Commons when it opened back up and other interests took our attention. Our first attempt at a business plan was a bit rough but I'm still proud of it!

  • Company: Finn's Fries
  • Date: Fall 2015
